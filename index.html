LOGLINE

To overcome a fear of the dark before marrying her fiancé, a woman must confront the darker truth behind her phobia, before it destroys their relationship.

DIRECTOR'S STATEMENT

Fear Hierarchy came to me at a time when I deeply understood Zoe.

As a survivor of sexual abuse, I was drawn to her paralysis around intimacy. The idea of being frozen in time—of remembering every moment you want to forget—resonated profoundly with me.

Zoe's secret becomes physical in this film. The darkness is not supernatural. It is memory.

This project is an offering to my past self. Healing is possible—but only after you face your demons.

— Tina Reeves

FEAR
HIERARCHY

Zoe is preparing for marriage while battling an intense fear of the dark. Using exposure therapy, she gradually dims the lights each night—but as darkness encroaches, suppressed childhood memories resurface.

The guest bedroom transforms into a distorted version of her past. In the final act, Zoe chooses to stand in the dark on her own terms—reclaiming power over the memory that once froze her in time.

The film reframes darkness not as terror, but as agency.
